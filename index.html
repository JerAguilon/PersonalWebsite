About me

Currently I'm a second year in Georgia Tech's Computer Science program, set to graduate May 2018. I self-taught myself Java out of curiosity in high school and have been hooked since.

If I had to pick my favorite personal project, it's hands down my Icon-Based Asthma Action Plan, which I did for research out in Tech Square. As somebody who was constantly troubled by the disease, I find value in knowing that my work could measurably improve the lives of others around me.

I have a lot of uncommon interests, but my best "party trick" is the fact that by the time you finish reading this sentence, I could have solved an entire Rubik's Cube.

Experience

Researcher
Full stack developer

Asthma is a chronic condition that afflicts 8.6% of all children, with a higher rate for those of low socioeconomic status. One of the most effective ways for parents to manage their childrens' asthma is through an Asthma Action Plan. Research has shown that icon-based plans have improved the health care management of those with lower reading comprehension. My role under Dr. Rosa Arriaga was to implement the backend of the IBAAP with the front end prototype.

Outreach Programming Teacher
Middle School Level
